### 🔬 Core Technical Innovation
How do you prevent a supply chain bottleneck from failing when LLM inference latency takes too long? **You separate the math from the semantics.** This project evolves traditional Digital Twins from passive monitoring tools into **Neuro-Symbolic Autonomous Swarms**, utilizing a dual-layer Edge computing architecture:

* ⚡ **The Fast Loop (Symbolic Layer):** A natively coded Java Genetic Algorithm handles Decentralized Swarm Arbitration. It evaluates stochastic parameter drift (Yield vs. Drift) and physically actuates the twin to isolate hazards in **2 to 12 milliseconds**.
* 🧠 **The Slow Loop (Neural Layer):** A localized Llama 3 8B model acts as the Plant Manager. Waking up hourly, it reads the macro-state data to generate semantic executive reports, ensuring strict Article 12 (Logging) and Article 13 (Transparency) compliance with the EU AI Act.

### 📊 Quantifiable Impact (Stress-Test Results)
Under severe stochastic conditions (80% failure probability spikes), the MADO-X framework autonomously achieved:
* **Hyper-Reactive Latency:** Reduced decision latency from ~150 seconds (Pure LLM) to an average of **2 to 5 milliseconds**.
* **Bottleneck Resilience:** Maintained a **98% Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE)** on the bottleneck asset (M3) by dynamically prioritizing preventative maintenance.
* **Data Sovereignty:** 100% offline, localized processing with immutable JSON-based audit trails.
